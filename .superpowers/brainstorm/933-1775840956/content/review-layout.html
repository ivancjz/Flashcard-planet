Human Review UI — Layout Options

How should the review queue and resolution workflow be structured?

A

Queue list + inline action buttons

Each row in the queue shows the listing title, AI guess, confidence, and three buttons (Accept / Override / Dismiss) directly on the row. Override opens a small inline search box below the row. No modal — everything happens in place.

Queue
Charizard VMAX 074/100 PSA 10
Guess: Charizard VMAX · Conf: 0.48 · ai_confidence_below_threshold
Umbreon VMAX Alt Art 215/264
Guess: Umbreon VMAX · Conf: 0.51 · ai_confidence_below_threshold

Pros

  • Fast — one click to accept or dismiss
  • All context visible at once

Cons

  • Override search clutters the list
  • Tight on small screens
B

Queue list + resolution modal

The queue shows compact rows. Clicking any row opens a focused modal with the full listing details, AI guess info, and the three action buttons. Override shows a search box inside the modal.

Queue
Charizard VMAX 074/100 PSA 10
Conf: 0.48 · queued 2h ago
→
Resolve: Umbreon VMAX Alt Art 215/264
AI guess: Umbreon VMAX (0.51 confidence)
Reason: ai_confidence_below_threshold

Pros

  • Focused resolution flow
  • Override search has full space
  • Clean queue — no clutter

Cons

  • Extra click to open each item
C

Split panel — queue left, detail right

Left panel is the scrollable queue. Clicking a row loads its full detail + action panel on the right without a modal or page reload. Persistent two-column layout.
